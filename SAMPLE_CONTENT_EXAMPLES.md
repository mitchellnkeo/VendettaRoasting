## üìù Sample Blog Post

**Title:** How to Brew the Perfect Pour-Over Coffee

**Excerpt:**
```
Learn the art of pour-over coffee brewing with our step-by-step guide. Discover the techniques 
that will elevate your morning coffee routine.
```

**Content:**
```
# The Art of Pour-Over Coffee

Pour-over coffee brewing is one of the most rewarding ways to make coffee at home. It gives you 
complete control over every aspect of the brewing process, allowing you to extract the perfect 
flavors from your beans.

## What You'll Need

- Freshly roasted coffee beans
- Pour-over dripper (Hario V60, Chemex, or Kalita Wave)
- Paper filter
- Gooseneck kettle
- Scale (for precise measurements)
- Timer

## Step-by-Step Guide

### Step 1: Measure Your Coffee

For a single cup, use 20-25 grams of coffee beans. Grind them to a medium-fine consistency, 
similar to sea salt.

### Step 2: Heat Your Water

Heat filtered water to 195-205¬∞F (90-96¬∞C). The exact temperature depends on your coffee's 
roast level - lighter roasts prefer higher temperatures.

### Step 3: Rinse the Filter

Place the filter in your dripper and rinse it with hot water. This removes any paper taste 
and preheats your brewing vessel.

### Step 4: Bloom

Add just enough water to saturate the coffee grounds (about twice the weight of your coffee). 
Let it bloom for 30 seconds. This allows the coffee to release carbon dioxide and prepare 
for extraction.

### Step 5: Pour in Circles

Slowly pour the remaining water in a circular motion, starting from the center and moving 
outward. Keep the water level consistent and avoid pouring directly on the filter.

### Step 6: Enjoy!

Your pour-over should be ready in 2-3 minutes. Pour into your favorite mug and enjoy the 
fruits of your labor!

## Pro Tips

- Use a scale to ensure consistency
- Keep your pour steady and controlled
- Experiment with grind size to find your perfect cup
- Clean your equipment after each use

Happy brewing!
```

## üí° Demo Tips

When demonstrating:

1. **Start with Announcements** - They're the simplest and show immediate results
2. **Then do Events** - Shows how to add images and structured data
3. **FAQs next** - Demonstrates categorization
4. **Blog Post** - Shows the rich text editor capabilities
5. **Homepage/About** - Shows how to update main page content

**Pro Tip:** Create the content in Sanity Studio first, then show how it appears on the website. This demonstrates the full workflow!
